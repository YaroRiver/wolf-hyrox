### WB Race Execution (critical final station)

**Setup:** Pick your target spot on the wall. Place chalk if allowed. Breathe 5 times before starting.

**Set 1 — 35 to 40 reps:**
- Start at 2.1 sec/rep rhythm — count "one-two" every rep
- Exhale UP (throw), inhale DOWN (squat) — this rhythm locks in by rep 5
- Eyes: fixed on top edge of target. Never track the ball.
- Power: legs and hips drive, arms guide
- Stop at 35–40 while still CLEAN — do not wait for form to break

**Deliberate reset — 10–15 sec:**
- Set the ball down. Hands on thighs. 5 full diaphragm breaths. Back straight.
- Pick up the ball. Reset position. Start immediately.

**Set 2 — 20–25 reps:**
- Same rhythm and breathing
- Stop when clean — not when you're struggling

**Short reset — 8–10 sec:**
- Same protocol

**Set 3 and finish — remainder reps:**
- Grind with technique. Squat depth matters — judges watch.
- Every rep must clear the target. Under-throws are no-reps.
- Finish strong.

**Target total time: 5:00–5:15**

**Buffer to 1:15:00: ~11:35 minutes.** Target is achievable even with meaningful station degradation.

**The race is lost by:**
1. Going out too fast on Run 1 → HR spirals → all stations cost more
2. Blowing up on WB trying for 60+ unbroken → collapse, time lost, cognitive shutdown
3. Panicking on Ski Erg or Row Erg and sprinting → HR spike

**The race is won by:**
1. Controlled Run 1 — find position, hold pace
2. Discipline on WB break pattern — lock in race strategy, no deviation
3. Staying aerobic on Ski Erg and Row Erg — these should feel like "breathing" stations
